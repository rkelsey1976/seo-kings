## Strategy — read this before scheduling

**GBP posts don't beat distance in the map pack.** Google filters local results by proximity to
the searcher, and a post won't override that. From Culvers Road:

- **East Bristol** (St George, Easton, Totterdown, Brislington) — 10–15 min. Genuine map pack
  chances. Worth real effort.
- **Gloucester Road corridor** (Bishopston, Horfield, Redland, Cotham) — 20 min. Map pack is a
  stretch; these earn their keep on organic and brand recognition.
- **North fringe** (Filton, Bradley Stoke, Yate) — 25–35 min. Effectively an organic and
  awareness play. Don't expect map pack movement and don't judge the posts on it.
- **Clifton and central** — 20–25 min, and the most competitive patch in the city with agencies
  based on the doorstep. Longest game of the lot.

**What this means practically:** phase 1 is where the enquiries come from. Phases 2–4 build
Bristol-wide presence and feed the organic pages, which is a slower and less direct return.
Set expectations accordingly rather than measuring everything against Keynsham results.

**Your strongest Bristol credential is that you rank #1 organically for "SEO Bristol".** That is
a verifiable claim about the most competitive local SEO term in the South West, and it does more
work than any pricing line. It's used in several posts below — keep it accurate, and drop it
from these posts immediately if the ranking moves.

## Phasing

Post weekly. Run a phase, then move on — don't interleave, so each area gets a clean run.

| Phase | Weeks | Areas | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — East** | 1–3 | St George, Easton, Totterdown | Closest, best map pack odds. Start here. |
| **2 — Gloucester Rd** | 4–6 | Bishopston, Horfield, Redland & Cotham | Highest commercial density. Organic play. |
| **3 — North fringe** | 7–9 | Filton, Bradley Stoke, Yate | Awareness. B2B and new-build angles. |
| **4 — Central** | 10–12 | Clifton, City Centre & Redcliffe, Montpelier & Stokes Croft | Hardest. Brand positioning. |

After week 12, cycle back to phase 1 rather than starting a fifth phase — the near areas are
where the work actually is.
